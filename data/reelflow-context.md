# Part 1: ReelFlow — General Product Context

## 1. What is ReelFlow?

ReelFlow is a platform that transforms websites into **interactive, video-led experiences**.

It turns static pages into **guided journeys**, helping visitors explore content, understand offerings, and make decisions more easily.

ReelFlow enables businesses to:
- Guide visitors instead of leaving them to self-navigate
- Communicate through short-form video instead of text
- Create more engaging and human website experiences

It replaces passive browsing with **interactive, self-guided discovery**.

## 2. What is the ReelFlow Product?

ReelFlow is a **conversion and engagement layer for websites**.

It sits on top of an existing website and adds interactive video experiences without requiring a rebuild.

The product provides an **end-to-end system** including:
- Planning and structuring content
- Creating video
- Building interactive flows
- Publishing to the website
- Tracking engagement and performance

It is not:
- A website builder
- A chatbot
- A simple video embed tool

It is a **guided journey system powered by interactive video**.

## 3. Product Components & Definitions

### Flows

A **Flow** is an interactive, branching video journey built on a single page of the website.

Flows can:
- Contain multiple short video clips
- Allow users to choose what to watch next
- Guide users toward relevant actions (e.g. demo, sign-up, registration)

Unlike linear video, flows are **interactive and decision-based**.

### Interactive Video Player

The ReelFlow player is the interface shown on the website. It:
- Displays video content
- Enables user interaction (choices, navigation, buttons)
- Guides users through different flows across the site

It can be embedded via a **lightweight install (e.g. single line of code)**.

### Overlay Player

The **Overlay Player** sits on top of the existing website as a separate, guided experience. It:
- Appears as a layer over the page (e.g. modal, widget, or triggered experience)
- Can be launched based on user behaviour (e.g. page load, click, scroll)
- Temporarily becomes the primary focus of the user's attention

This format is best for:
- Introducing a guided journey quickly
- Capturing attention on high-value pages
- Driving users into a structured flow without relying on page layout

The overlay approach allows ReelFlow to **add a high-impact experience to any site instantly**, without needing changes to page structure or design.

### Inline Player

The **Inline Player** is embedded directly within the page content. It:
- Sits naturally within the layout (e.g. within a section, hero, or content block)
- Feels like part of the website rather than a separate layer
- Allows users to engage with video flows as they scroll and explore

This format is best for:
- Enhancing specific pages (e.g. product, pricing, landing pages)
- Supporting existing content rather than interrupting it
- Creating a more seamless, integrated experience

Inline deployment makes ReelFlow feel like a **native part of the website experience**, rather than an overlay on top of it.

### Video Content

ReelFlow uses **short-form video (typically ~20–90 seconds)**. Video can be:
- Recorded by team members
- Professionally produced
- AI-generated

The purpose is to:
- Replace text-heavy explanations
- Build trust through human presence
- Improve clarity and engagement

### AI Video Stand-In

The **AI Video Stand-In** allows users to create realistic, human-like video without needing to be on camera. It enables:
- Scalable video creation without repeated filming
- Consistent presentation across flows
- Faster production of new content

This is particularly useful for:
- Teams without access to regular filming
- Scaling content across multiple pages or campaigns
- Keeping video content up to date with minimal effort

### Flow Builder

The Flow Builder allows users to:
- Create and structure flows
- Connect video clips into journeys
- Design branching paths based on user intent

Flows can be built manually or generated / assisted by AI.

### Analytics & Insights

ReelFlow tracks how users interact with flows. This includes:
- Which paths users choose
- What topics they engage with
- How far they progress

This provides **high-intent behavioural data**, not just pageviews.

## ICP 1: Event Organisers 🎪

> **Status: Primary ICP — April 2026.** This is ReelFlow's most developed audience. Use this section whenever generating content for event organiser audiences, event industry channels, or event-specific landing pages.

### A. Who Are Event Organisers?

Event organisers are B2B companies that plan, produce and market professional events — including trade shows, conferences, summits, expos, and exhibitions.

They are responsible for:
- Selling sponsorship and exhibition packages to brands and businesses
- Driving delegate registrations and ticket sales
- Attracting and managing speakers and keynotes
- Building and maintaining an engaged community year-round — not just around the event itself

They operate **high-stakes, time-sensitive campaigns** where the event date is a hard deadline and website performance directly impacts revenue.

**Types of event organisers ReelFlow serves:**
- **Large-scale trade show organisers** (e.g. EasyFairs, running multiple portfolio events)
- **Single-event organisers** running one flagship annual conference or summit
- **Boutique B2B conference organisers** with niche, high-value audiences
- **Association-led events** where the event is a key membership and revenue driver

**Example customers:**
- **EasyFairs** — Paris Packaging Week 2026, Advanced Engineering
- **Event Tech Live** — annual B2B event tech conference
- **SaaStock** — B2B SaaS conference
- **HigherIN**
- **Gripped**

### B. Why Event Organisers Are the Right ICP

Event organisers are an ideal fit because of a structural mismatch in their market: **their product is inherently human, energetic and experiential — but their digital presence is static and faceless.**

**Multiple audiences, one website**
Event organisers must simultaneously serve radically different personas — attendees, exhibitors, sponsors, and speakers — each with different questions, motivations and conversion goals. A static website cannot efficiently serve all of them. ReelFlow's branching flows allow each persona to be guided down a tailored journey from a single page.

**High conversion pressure**
Event revenue is directly tied to registrations and sponsor/exhibitor sign-ups. Every lost website visitor is a measurable commercial loss. This makes organisers acutely aware of conversion performance and receptive to tools that demonstrably improve it.

**Video is natural for events**
Events are inherently visual and people-driven. Video of show floors, speakers, and event directors is a natural fit — and organisers often already have video assets or access to the people who can create them.

**Year-round engagement problem**
Most event websites are only maintained in the weeks around the event. ReelFlow enables organisers to maintain a compelling, trust-building digital presence 365 days a year — shortening the research cycle no matter when a visitor arrives.

**The "static brochure" problem is acute**
Event websites are notorious for being PDF prospectus equivalents — walls of text, sponsor tiers, speaker bios, agenda PDFs. This format is deeply misaligned with how modern buyers research and decide. ReelFlow replaces this with an interactive, human-led experience.

### C. The Problems We Solve for Event Organisers

**Problem 1: The Multi-Audience Challenge**
Event websites must serve radically different personas simultaneously — a potential exhibitor wants to know about ROI and audience quality; a delegate wants to know if it's worth their time; a speaker wants to know about the platform and audience fit. Static websites cannot route these users efficiently. The result is confusion, friction, and drop-off.

*ReelFlow solution:* Interactive branching flows that ask visitors who they are and route them to the most relevant content and CTA within seconds.

**Problem 2: Text-Heavy, Passive Websites**
Modern event audiences — particularly in B2B — are video-native and time-poor. Yet most event websites are wall-to-wall text: long prospectus pages, PDF downloads, agenda tables, and generic "why attend" copy. This creates friction and fails to communicate the energy and value of the event.

*ReelFlow solution:* Short-form video (20–90 seconds) replaces text explanations. Visitors watch rather than read, and the event's energy is communicated through real people — the event director, past speakers, happy exhibitors.

**Problem 3: The Trust Deficit**
In a world of AI-generated content and generic marketing, faceless websites struggle to build the personal trust needed to drive a high-value decision like committing to a sponsorship package or booking a ticket. Sponsors especially need to feel confident in the organiser before they will part with significant budget.

*ReelFlow solution:* Human-led video puts real faces — the Event Director, keynote speakers, past exhibitors — at the centre of the experience. This builds rapport and credibility far faster than any written content can.

**Problem 4: Sponsor Enquiries Rely on Cold Outreach**
Many event organisers still rely heavily on outbound sales to fill sponsorship and exhibition packages. Their website does little to pre-qualify or nurture inbound interest — because the static prospectus page doesn't communicate value effectively.

*ReelFlow solution:* A dedicated "Why Sponsor" flow, featuring the Event Director, replaces the static PDF prospectus with an interactive video experience. It communicates audience quality, buying power, matchmaking ROI, and brand opportunities — all before the first sales conversation. This warms leads and shortens the sales cycle.

**Problem 5: Low Delegate Conversion — New and Returning**
First-time visitors need to understand quickly whether the event is right for them. Returning attendees need a reason to come back. Both groups are underserved by typical event websites, which present the same generic content to everyone.

*ReelFlow solution:* A "Curated Value" flow that branches by persona — new delegates receive a 30–60 second event overview; returning delegates see a "What's New" video highlighting evolved formats, fresh speakers, and new content to overcome "been there, done that" inertia.

**Problem 6: Attendee Engagement Starts Too Late**
Most attendee engagement begins in the days before the event, through reminder emails and agenda PDFs. This is a missed opportunity — the website visit months in advance is when excitement and commitment can be built.

*ReelFlow solution:* An "Experience Optimiser" flow lets registered attendees explore the agenda by problem statement, meet speakers via 30-second intros, and plan their visit — all through short-form, authentic video. Attendees arrive more prepared, more excited, and more engaged.

### D. ReelFlow's Value Proposition for Event Organisers

**Core proposition:** ReelFlow transforms static event websites into human-led, interactive video experiences that guide every visitor — whether exhibitor, sponsor, delegate, or speaker — toward the action that matters most.

**For sponsors and exhibitors:** Replace the PDF prospectus with a dynamic, video-led "Why Exhibit" journey that builds trust and communicates ROI before the first sales call. Increase inbound enquiries and shorten the sales cycle.

**For delegates (new):** Answer "Is this event for me?" in under 60 seconds. Communicate energy, relevance, and community through authentic video — driving faster registration decisions and reducing drop-off.

**For delegates (returning):** Combat fatigue and inertia with a "What's New" flow that surfaces the freshest reasons to return — new formats, speakers, content tracks — through video storytelling rather than a reworded "what to expect" page.

**For the organiser's brand:** Build trust and emotional connection with your audience year-round — not just in the weeks around the show — through a consistent, human digital presence.
