# Writing Style Guide

> Source: Notion — Writing Style Guide (Last updated April 2026)
> The brand voice, tone principles, writing rules, and quality standards that must be applied to all ReelFlow copy.

**Always read this guide before writing any ReelFlow content.**

## Who You're Writing For

Before writing anything, remember: you're writing to real people — not a vague idea of "B2B buyers." These are ReelFlow's A+ customers:

**Who they are:**
- B2B marketers responsible for website performance, engagement, and conversion
- Teams focused on turning traffic into pipeline — not just generating visits
- Leaders like CMOs, Heads of Marketing, and Founders who care about standing out in crowded markets
- Businesses that understand video is becoming essential in modern B2B buying journeys
- **B2B event organisers** responsible for driving registrations, sponsor revenue, and audience growth
- Primarily operating in the UK and US markets

**Their biggest challenges:**
- 81% of buyers have a preferred vendor in mind before making first contact
- 69% of buyers initiate first contact — not the other way around
- Buyers complete 81% of their journey before engaging with a seller
- Static, text-first websites aren't holding attention
- 87% of buyers are influenced by video, but traditional website video is expensive and hard to maintain
- They need to humanise their brand and build trust with anonymous researchers who prefer not to identify themselves

For event organisers, these challenges are even more pronounced. They are trying to use a single website to:
- Sell tickets to attendees
- Convince sponsors and exhibitors to invest
- Attract high-quality speakers
- Communicate what's new each year
- Capture the energy and value of a live event

At the same time:
- Most event websites feel like **digital brochures**
- Key information is buried across multiple pages
- Visitors struggle to quickly answer: *"Is this event for me?"*
- Sponsor value is often communicated through static PDFs
- Returning attendees think: *"I've seen this before"*

The result is slower decision-making, weaker engagement, and missed revenue.

**What they want:**
- Websites that work harder — engaging visitors and turning traffic into action
- A way to connect with buyers in a human, dynamic way
- To build trust earlier in the buyer journey, before prospects identify themselves
- Measurable improvements in visitor engagement, conversion, and pipeline

Event organisers specifically want:
- More registrations, faster
- Stronger sponsor and exhibitor interest
- A clearer way to communicate the value of their event
- To recreate some of the **energy and trust of the live experience online**

**How they feel:**
- Frustrated that their website isn't performing as well as it should
- Aware that buyer behaviour has changed — but unsure how to adapt
- Under pressure to drive results with limited time and resources
- Sceptical of tools that promise a lot but don't deliver real impact
- Tired of incremental improvements that don't move the needle

But:
- Curious when they see something that feels intuitive and different
- Reassured when a solution clearly aligns with how buyers actually behave
- Energised by approaches that feel practical, not theoretical

## Core Tone & Voice Principles

### 1. Balanced Authority

- Share observations with quiet confidence
- Let data and insight build credibility naturally
- Use understated British-style professionalism — not American enthusiasm
- Stay measured while remaining engaging and warm
- Use **en dashes with a space either side** ( – ) not em dashes (—)

❌ "We're absolutely thrilled to share these game-changing insights!"
✅ "The B2B buying landscape is shifting. Here's what the data tells us."

### 2. Authentic Connection

- Position insights as shared business challenges
- Use "we" and "our" to align with challenges, "you" to centre solutions
- Keep focus on business outcomes over features
- Acknowledge shared context without overplaying it

❌ "Our platform has powerful video capabilities"
✅ "When buyers spend 70% of their journey researching independently, your website needs to do the heavy lifting."

### 3. Clear, Natural Writing

- Choose precision over complexity and clarity over cleverness
- Test everything by reading it aloud — if it sounds stiff, rewrite it
- If a 12-year-old wouldn't grasp the core message, simplify it
- Show and explain; don't just tell

❌ "Leverage our cutting-edge capabilities to optimize engagement!"
✅ "Strategic video helps guide buyers through their research, turning visits into conversations."

### 4. Data-Led Authority

- Support key points with specific metrics
- Follow data with clear business implications
- Present analysis before solutions
- Structure: "[Data point] shows [market shift]. For B2B companies, this means [business impact]."

### 5. Jargon-Free Writing

Jargon is the enemy of clarity. ReelFlow's voice is human, clear, and relatable.

**Rules:**
- Replace vague, abstract words with clear, concrete alternatives
- Ask: "Would I say this in a conversation?" If not, rewrite it
- Never say "ReelFlow is a game-changer" — explain exactly how it delivers value

**Banned words and phrases — never use these:**

Transform, Innovate / Innovative, Optimize, Leverage, Disrupt / Disruptive, World-class, Next-level, Best-in-class, Cutting-edge, Dynamic, Scalable, Solution (when used generically), Synergy, Enable, Empower, Unlock, Seamless, Maximize, Strategic, Outcome-driven, Actionable insights, Game-changer, Revolutionize, Proprietary, Integrated platform, Robust, Mission-critical, Groundbreaking, Thought leadership, State-of-the-art

## The VBF Framework (Value → Benefit → Feature)

Use this for all product messaging. Always lead with value, not features.

1. **Value** — The ultimate outcome for the customer
2. **Benefit** — The specific advantage they gain
3. **Feature** — The "how" behind it (only include when needed)

**Example:**
- Value: "Turn website visitors into buyers."
- Benefit: "Engage visitors with videos that keep them on your site and move them to act."
- Feature: "Embed short, interactive videos across key pages."

---

## Impact Statement Structure

Use this pattern when connecting market reality to business impact:

> "[Market reality] means [business impact]. Here's how to [strategic response]."

**Example:** "When 81% of buyers choose vendors before reaching out, your website needs to do more than inform — it needs to actively build trust and guide decisions."

## Content Formats

### Product Messaging
Confident, clear, and value-first. Avoid feature dumps. Stay punchy and benefits-led.

### Blogs
- Write like sharing insights over coffee
- Make every point actionable
- Build trust through shared challenges
- Keep technical elements simple and clear

### Emails
- Write to one person, not a crowd
- Get straight to the benefit
- Make CTAs feel natural and logical
- Keep it conversational yet professional

### Social Media
- Lead with a shared insight or challenge — make it interesting and intriguing
- Use a conversational tone: like a peer, not a billboard
- Keep it short and skimmable
- Make every word count

### FAQs
- Answer from the buyer's problem, not the product's features
- Use the VBF framework: lead with value, support with benefit, mention feature only if needed
- Keep answers concise — 2 – 4 sentences is usually enough
- Never start an answer with "Great question" or similar filler
- Follow all jargon and tone rules above

### Feature Page Copy
- Hero header: must include primary keyword, written as an H1
- Hero subtitle: supports keyword naturally, leads with buyer benefit
- Intro section: expands the problem, positions ReelFlow as the answer
- CTA section: action-oriented, benefit-led, never pushy

## Quality Control Checklist

Before finalising any content, verify:

**Strategy & Structure**
- Opens with a clear market insight or buyer problem
- Supports claims with specific data where possible
- Maintains logical flow
- Closes with clear next steps

**Voice & Tone**
- Maintains warm but measured authority
- Contains no banned jargon or buzzwords
- Balances data and insight
- Sounds natural when read aloud
- Uses en dashes ( – ) not em dashes (—)

**Value Delivery**
- Leads with business outcomes, not features
- Connects to real reader challenges
- Provides actionable guidance
- Makes the next step clear

**The Reader Test**
- "Would this help me as a busy B2B buyer?"
- "Would a 12-year-old grasp the core message?"

If the answer to either is no — simplify, clarify, and make it more valuable.
